This document describes the SA-342L Gazelle French Light Attack Helicopter.

The SA-342L is a export light attack variant of the SA-341/342 Gazelle.


The Eurocopter/Aerospatiale SA 341/342 Gazelle is a French built light utility helicopter which was first flown in 1967. Military missions include attack, antitank, anti helicopter, reconnaissance, utility, transport, and training. The three-blade main rotor is mounted on top of the fuselage at the rear of the cabin. The single turboshaft engine, mounted on top of the fuselage and to the rear of the rotor shaft, features a prominent, upturned exhaust. The teardrop-shaped fuselage has a round, glassed-in cockpit and landing skids. The tapering tail boom mid-mounted on the fuselage has a swept-back tail fin which is tapered with a square tip and rectangular flats with small fins. The fan rotor housing is built into the lower tail.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Angola, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Burundi, Cyprus, Ecuador, Egypt, France, Gabon, Iraq, Kuwait, Lebanon, Montenegro, Morocco, Qatar, Rwanda, Serbia, Tunisia, United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland.
