This document describes the BMD-2 Russian Airborne Amphibious Infantry Fighting Vehicle (IFV).

The BMD-2 is a Soviet-era airborne infantry fighting vehicle, introduced in 1985. It is a variant of BMD-1 with a new turret and some changes done to the hull. BMD stands for Boyevaya Mashina Desanta (Боевая Машина Десанта, which literally translates to "Airborne Combat Vehicle"). It was developed as a replacement for BMD-1 but it failed to replace it completely because of the downfall of the Soviet economy in the 1980s.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: CFE Treaty, Russian Federation, Ukraine, Uzbekistan.

Here is Variants information for the BMD-2 Russian Airborne Amphibious Infantry Fighting Vehicle (IFV):
The BMD-2 is The BMD-2 has a box-shaped hull with a gently-sloped glacis and steep belly plate from an angular nose. The sides of the hull are vertical and there is a turret mounted in the middle of the hull roof. The front compartment of the vehicle houses the driver and a passenger; the middle turret and the rear provides space for dismounts..
The BMD-2K is The BMD-2K is a variant of the BMD-2 designed to perform as a command vehicle. It carries additional communications equipment so that it can command units at the platoon and company level..
The BMD-2-KU is The BMD-2-KU is an improved C2 vehicle based on the BMD-2K. It is designed to provide a modernised vehicle that can be used to better control a battalion-sized formation..
The BMD-2K-AU is The BMD-2K-AU is a modified command vehicle based on the BMD-2K..
The BMD-2M is The BMD-2M is an upgraded BMD-2. It is a vehicle modernized with improved mission systems so that the vehicle’s combat capabilities can be improved without replacing the turret..
