This document describes the CH-47J Japanese Medium Transport Helicopter.

The CH-47J is a medium-transport helicopter for the Japan Ground Self-Defense Force (JGSDF), and the Japan Air Self-Defense Force (JASDF).[95] The differences between the CH-47J and the CH-47D are the engine, rotor brake and avionics, for use for general transportation, SAR and disaster activity like U.S. forces. The CH-47J is produced by Kawasaki Heavy Industries under a license by Boeing. It is equipped with a global positioning system (GPS), FLIR, and PNVG.

The CH-47JA, introduced in 1993, is a long-range version of the CH-47J, fitted with enlarged saddle fuel tank, a nose radar, an AAQ-16 FLIR in a turret under the nose, and a partial glass cockpit. Both versions are built under license in Japan by Kawasaki Heavy Industries, who produced 61 aircraft by April 2001.
