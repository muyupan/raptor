This document describes the K-7 South Korean 9mm Submachine Gun.

The K-7 South Korean 9mm Submachine Gun is a modern submachine gun of South Korean origin. It was developed by Daewoo in the early 2000s as a less expensive alternative to the proven and widely used Heckler & Koch MP5SD. The K-7 is a very simple weapon and some parts are shared with the older South Korean K1A assault rifle.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Bangladesh, Indonesia, South Korea (Republic of Korea), Thailand.

Here is System information for the K-7 South Korean 9mm Submachine Gun:
The Alternate Designation is K-7.
The Type is Silenced Submachine Gun.
The Caliber is 9 mm.
The Manufacturer is Daewoo Telecom.
The Action is Gas operated, simple blowback.
The Rate of Fire is 1,100 rounds/min.
The Muzzle Velocity is 295 m/s.
The Effective Firing Range is 150 m.
The Feed System is 30-round (30-round magazines can also be used from Uzi or 32-round magazines from Beretta PM12s).
The Sights is Iron sights, tritium post front and aperture rear.
The Suppressor is The suppressor reduces the pressure of the gas produced during discharge, and by extension, reduces the amount of noise generated. In addition, the suppressor significantly distorts the sound of the gunshot so that it is very difficult to pinpoint the location of the shooter. Per shot, the mean average of noise generated by the K7 is approximately 111.5 dB.

Here is Dimensions information for the K-7 South Korean 9mm Submachine Gun:
The Length, Stock Extended is 788 mm.
The Length, Stock Retracted is 606 mm.
The Barrel Length is 134 mm.
The Weight is 3.4 kg with empty magazine.

Here is Ammunition information for the K-7 South Korean 9mm Submachine Gun:
The Type is Rifle.
The Caliber is 9 mm.
The Cartridge is 9x19mm Parabellum.
