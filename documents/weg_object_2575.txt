This document describes the CM-21 Taiwanese Tracked Infantry Fighting Vehicle (IFV).

The CM-21 Taiwanese Tracked Infantry Fighting Vehicle (IFV)is an armored vehicle designed and manufactured by the Republic of China Armored Vehicle Development Center, based on the United States' model M113 APC. The first prototype was manufactured in 1979, and the CM-21 officially entered service in 1982.

The CM-21 is still in use today, with over 1,000 units manufactured and a number of different variants.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Taiwan.
