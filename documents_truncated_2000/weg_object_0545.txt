This document describes the L16A1 British 81mm Mortar.

The L16A1 was developed in 1957 as a replacement for the British army's 3-in (76-mm) World War II-era mortar. The lightweight, accurate, drop-fired weapon is now the standard mortar for the British armed forces and has been exported to a large number of other countries.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Bahrain, Belize, Cameroon, Fiji, Guyana, Jamaica, Japan, Kenya, Kuwait, Malawi, Malaysia, Netherlands, New Zealand, Nigeria, Norway, Oman, Qatar, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, Zimbabwe.

Here is System information for the L16A1 British 81mm Mortar:
The Alternate Designation is L16A1.
The Type is Mortar.
The Caliber is 81 mm.
The Manufacturer is Initial operational capability (IOC) was achieved in 1961. The L16A1 is in service in numerous countries.
A modified L16 is also in service with the U.S. Army and Marine Corps as the M252 (see separate database entry)..
The Crew is 5.
The Bore is smoothbore.
The Elevation is 800 mils to 1,515 mils.
The Traverse at 800 Mils is 125 mils to either side.
The Traverse at 1,515 Mils is 325 mils to either side.
The Sustained Rate of Fire is 15 Rounds per Minute.
The Muzzle Velocity is 225 m/s.
The Maximum Range, Charge 1 is 500 m.
The Maximum Range, Charge 6 is 4,500 m.
The Maximum Range, Charge 8 is 5,650 m.
The Minimum Range, Charge 1 is 100 m.
The Minimum Range, Charge 6 is 1,600 m.
