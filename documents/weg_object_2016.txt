This document describes the Type 75 Chinese 105mm Recoilless Gun.

The Type 75 105mm recoilless gun was developed in the mid-1970s for the PLA. Mounted on a Beijing BJ2020S (BJ212) 4X4 lightweight vehicle, the Type 75 is designed to provide a lightweight, self-propelled direct/indirect fire against enemy personnel, field fortifications, and light armored vehicles. The weapon was fielded by the PLA ground forces in mass numbers, with a considerable number of them still in active service today.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: China.

The Type is Recoilless Gun.
The Manufacturer is NORINCO - China North Industries Group Corporation - CNGC.
The Chassis is Beijing BJ2020S (BJ212) 4X4 lightweight vehicle.
The Configuration is 4x4.
The Crew is 5.
The Airlift Capability is The Type 75 is widely equipped by the PLA airborne forces due to its light weight. The vehicle can be airlifted by medium transport helicopters and fixed-wing transport aircraft, but it cannot be air-dropped by parachutes..
The Note is The Type 75 is effective against enemy personnel, bunkers, fortifications, and light armoured vehicles. The weapon can be used for either direct or indirect fire. Its shape-charge warhead has limited armour penetration capability, thought it poses little or no threat to heavy armour target such as main battle tanks (MBT)..
