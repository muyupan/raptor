This document describes the BTR-70 (BTR M1978) Russian 8x8 Amphibious Armored Personnel Carrier (APC).

The BTR-70 is a Russian-made 8x8 armored vehicle personnel carrier which was shown for the first time to the public in November 1980 during a military parade in Moscow. The vehicle was originally developed during the late 1960s under the industrial designator GAZ-4905. The BTR-70 enters in service with the Russian army in August 1972. The BTR-70 was widely used during the Soviet war in Afghanistan and many smaller conflicts in Eastern Europe. After the Cold War these AFV saw service with the UN in the Balkans with high success.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bangladesh, Belarus, Bosnia and Herzegovina, CFE Treaty, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Macedonia, Mali, Mexico, Mongolia, Pakistan, Russian Federation, Sierra Leone, Sudan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, Uzbekistan, Zambia.

Here is Variants information for the BTR-70 (BTR M1978) Russian 8x8 Amphibious Armored Personnel Carrier (APC):
The BTR-70 is The BTR-70 is the base variant of the family. It is an APC designed to traverse a fire zone with a section of infantry dismounts and provide them with limited fire support from the rear..
The BTR-70MB is The BTR-70MB is an upgraded BTR-70 that is in service with an undisclosed country. It is essentially the same as the BTR-70 and designed to perform the same function..
The BTR-70MB1 is The BTR-70MB1 is based on the BTR-70MB with some modifications for Belarusian service, it is intended to replace older APCs such as the BTR-60PB and the BTR-70..
The BTR-70V is This variant was also known as the BTR-70 M1986/1 and has a reworked troop compartment that can be used to transport two 30 mm AGS-17 AGLs or stretchers. It can carry a total of 10 infantrymen or eight infantrymen and two 30 mm ASG-17 AGLs..
The BTR-70MS is This is a turretless communications vehicle with the MS standing for Mashina Svyazi (signals vehicle). Its status is uncertain..
The BTR-70 KShM is This is a command/staff vehicle. The KShM stands for Komandno-Shtabnaya Mashina. It was originally fitted with the R-145BM communications system but it is unclear if this is retained in any vehicles that remain in service..
The TAB-77 is The TAB-77 is a license-manufactured version of the BTR-70 manufactured in Romania..
The BTR-70Kh is This was developed by Russia for the then East German Army and was also referred to as the SPW-70Ch..
The BTR-70MBP is This was the successor to the escort vehicle BTR-60MBP (Mashina Bojevogo Posta), and was also found in strategic missile units..
The BTR-70D is The prototypes that used diesel fuel instead of gasoline were designated as the BTR-70D. This is also the

The BTR-70DI is The BTR-70DI is a Ukrainian BTR-70, but is powered by a Euro II 276 horsepower diesel engine..
