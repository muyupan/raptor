This document describes the BTR-3U Ukrainian 8x8 Amphibious Armored Personnel Carrier (APC).

The BTR-3U Ukrainian 8x8 Amphibious Armored Personnel Carrier (APC) was jointly developed in 2000 by Ukrainian and Emirati companies as a private venture. It can be seen as an improved variant of the Soviet BTR-80 APC.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Azerbaijan, Chad, Ecuador, Iraq, Kazakhstan, Myanmar, Nigeria, Thailand, Ukraine, United Arab Emirates.

Here is Main Weapon System information for the BTR-3U Ukrainian 8x8 Amphibious Armored Personnel Carrier (APC):
The Armament Module is 1 x Shkval Armament Module. The Shkval is an integrated, modular weapon station intended for application on a wide range of armored vehicles. It was developed by the State Scientific Technical Centre of Artillery & Rifle Arms of Ukraine. The Shkval turret includes KBA-2/ZTM-1 30mm cannon, KT-7.62 7.62mm coaxial machine gun, AGS-17 30mm automatic grenade launcher, and anti-tank missile launchers. Optionally its modular design allows replacement of guns, grenade launchers and missiles by others that better suit the customer's requirements. This weapon station has been designed for operation by a single person. The rotating Shkval weapon station carries 350 30mm rounds, 2,500 7.62mm rounds and 29 ready-to-fire grounds for the 30mm grenade launcher with an 89 additional rounds in reserve. The weapon station also carries two side-mounted wire-guided Red Arrow 8 (HJ-8) or Konkurs anti-tank missiles. The module is fitted with an stabilized fire control system with an operating range of 4,000 or 700 meters at daylight or night respectively. as well as six (3+3) 81mm smoke/aerosol grenade launchers..
