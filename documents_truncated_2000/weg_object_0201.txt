This document describes the YJ-18 (CH-SS-NX-13) Chinese Short-Range Anti-Ship Cruise Missile.

The YJ-18 (CH-SS-NX-13) Chinese Short-Range Anti-Ship Cruise Missile is a Chinese cruise missile with variants for antiship and land-attack missions. It is reportedly derived from the Russian 3M-54E “Klub” missile and entered service around 2014.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: China.

Here is Variants information for the YJ-18 (CH-SS-NX-13) Chinese Short-Range Anti-Ship Cruise Missile:
The YJ-18 is Original land-attack variant launched from ships..
The YJ-18A is Vertically-launched shipborne anti-ship variant, deployed aboard the Type 052D and Type 055 destroyers..
The YJ-18B is Submarine-launched variant..
The YJ-18C is Land-attack version launched from shipping containers similar to the Club-K missile system..
