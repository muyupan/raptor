This document describes the Hwasong-5 (Scud-B) North Korean Close-Range Ballistic Missile.

The Hwasong-5 (Scud-B) North Korean Close-Range Ballistic Missile is a North Korean short-range ballistic missile (SRBM). It is the DPRK variant of the Soviet-produced ‘Scud B‘, which North Korea purchased from Egypt and reverse-engineered.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Iran (Islamic Republic of), Myanmar, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea).

Here is Variants information for the Hwasong-5 (Scud-B) North Korean Close-Range Ballistic Missile:
The Shahab-1 is Iranian version of Hwasong-5.

Here is System information for the Hwasong-5 (Scud-B) North Korean Close-Range Ballistic Missile:
The Alternate Designation is Hwasong-5; NATO: Scud-B.
The Type is Short-Range Ballistic Missile.
The Manufacturer is 125 factory at Pyongyang, a research and development institute at Sanum-dong and the Musudan-ri Launch Facility..
The Launcher is Mobile truck transporter - erector.
The Crew is 5.
The Payload is Single warhead.
The Warhead is HE, potential chemical, biological, submunition payloads.
The Propulsion is Single-stage, liquid-fueled.
The Guidance System is Inertial system.
The Maximum Range is 300 km.
The CEP is 450 m.

Here is Dimensions information for the Hwasong-5 (Scud-B) North Korean Close-Range Ballistic Missile:
The Length is 10.94 m.
The Diameter is 0.88 m.
The Height is 2.67 m.
The Missile Weight is 5,860 kg.
The Warhead Weight is 985 kg.
