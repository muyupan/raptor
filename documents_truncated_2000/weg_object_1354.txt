This document describes the Altay-Class (Project 160 Class) Russian Medium Seagoing Tanker.

Altay-Class (Project 160 Class) Russian Medium Seagoing Tanker is a class of replenishment oiler built for the Soviet Navy between 1967 and 1972. The Altay-class vessels were built for the USSR by the Finnish shipbuilding yard Rauma-Repola. Over 60 vessels of this type were built for Soviet service, most with the fishing fleet and merchant fleet. Only six vessels were ordered for service with the Soviet Navy. Project 160 tankers can refuel one ship at a time from either side or over the stern

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Russian Federation.
