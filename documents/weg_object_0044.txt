This document describes the ZTZ-88 (Type 88) Chinese Main Battle Tank (MBT).

The ZTZ-88 (Type 88) Chinese Main Battle Tank (MBT) is an improved version of Type 80 that entered service in 1988 under the "Type 88" PLA designation. More than the former, it integrates a large number of Western technologies. The design is similar to the Type 80-II, to the extent of the front storage racks are removed to fit a set of explosive reactive armor (ERA) bricks. Production of Type 88-series MBTs was stopped in 1995 but about 400 to 500 Type 88/A/B are in service with the PLA today.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: China, Pakistan, Sudan, Uganda.
