This document describes the Baktar Shikan Pakistani Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM).

The Baktar Shikan Pakistani Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM) is visually identical to the Chinese NORINCO Red Arrow 8 (Hong Jain 8; HJ‚Äê8) guided-weapon system and is assumed to be the same weapon system produced in Pakistan under licence. From imagery and data, the original missile would seem to be equivalent to the Red Arrow 8A.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Bangladesh, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Pakistan, Serbia, Sri Lanka, Syria.

Here is System information for the Baktar Shikan Pakistani Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM):
The Alternative Designations is Baktar Shikan.
The Type is Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM).
The Manufacturer is The Dr. A Q Khan Research Laboratories are reported to have been involved in the early development of the Baktar Shikan, with production later undertaken by the Institute of Industrial Control Systems located in Rawalpindi..
The Crew is 3.
The Engine is Single-stage solid-propellant rocket.
The Guidance System is SACLOS wire-guidance.
The Firing Range is 3,000 m.
The Warhead is Standard and Tandem warheads.
The Armor Penetration, Standard Warhead is 500 mm.
The Armor Penetration, Tandem Warhead is 600 mm.
The Accessories is Laser aiming device, IR Goniometer.
