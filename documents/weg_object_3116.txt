This document describes the M1A1 Abrams American Main Battle Tank (MBT).

The M1A1 Abrams main battle tank was developed by General Dynamics. It is a further development of the M1 Abrams. The M1A1 was revealed in 1985 with production commencing the same year. It entered service with US Army in 1986. Production ended in 1992 and approximately 4 800 M1A1 Abrams tanks were built. Currently, US Army operates nearly 4 400 of these tanks, and US Marine Corps just over 400. It is planned that the fleet of refurbished and upgraded M1A1 tanks will remain with the US military until at least 2021. Export operators are Australia (59), Egypt (1005) and Iraq (140).

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Australia, Egypt, Iraq, Kuwait, Morocco, Saudi Arabia, United States of America.

The M1A1 is Production started in 1985 and continued to 1992, pressurized NBC system, rear bustle rack for improved stowage of supplies and crew belongings, redesigned blow-off panels and M256 120 mm smoothbore cannon (4,976 built for the U.S. Army, 221 for USMC, 59 M1A1 AIM SA sold to Australia)..
The M1A1 FEP USMC is The US Marine Corps (USMC) acquired M1A1s that were identical to the US Army counterpart, and has since upgraded them with a Firepower Enhancement Package (FEP)..
The M1A1SA; M1A1SA/ED; M1A1ED is The M1A1SA/ED became the main battle tank of the US Army following upgrades to the M1A1..
The M1A1, Australia is Australia operates a fleet of 59 M1A1s.
The M1A1M is An export variant ordered by the Iraqi Army with depleted uranium armor removed and older thermal imaging system with lower resolution used..
The M1A2 is The M1A2 is an improved M1A1 and includes a number of significant changes such as an independent commander’s sight, full digital electronic architecture and a ROWS..
The M1A2, Kuwait is Kuwait purchased the M1A2 in 1994 in a bid to reinforce its land forces following the Gulf War and the Western-style tank gives the Kuwaiti army the ability to be trained by US forces..
The M1A2, Saudi Arabia is The M1A2 in service with Saudi Arabia is a newbuild version..
The M1A2S, Saudi Arabia is The upgrade from M1A2 to the M1A2S standard is designed to facilitate easier operation of the M1A2 in combat and improve the vehicle’s combat capability and reliability..
The M1A2 SEP v1 is The M1A2 SEP v1 update ran concurrently to the AIM XXI refit as well as the overhaul that led to the M1A1SA/ED..
The M1A2 SEP v2 is The Systems Enhancement Package v2 (SEP v2) was, until 2017, the most recent operational upgrade for US M1A1 and M1A2 tanks..
The M1A2C (formerly SEP v3) is The M1A2C is the latest and most modern upgrade applied to M1 series vehicles in US service..
The M104 Wolverine is The Wolverine is the only horizontally launched armoured bridge in the US inventory..
The USMC Assault Breacher Vehicle (M1150) is The Assault Breacher Vehicle is an AEV built to clear a path through minefields that friendly forces can follow. It is based on an Abrams hull and is primarily in use with the USMC..
The Joint Assault Bridge XM1075 (JAB) is The Joint Assault Bridge was designed to provide the USMC and US Army with a vehicle launched bridge based on the hull of a modified M1 hull..
