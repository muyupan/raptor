This document describes the Beretta ARX-160 Italian 5.56 mm Assault Rifle.

The Beretta ARX-160 Italian 5.56 mm Assault Rifle was developed for the Italian armed forces as a part of the Soldato Futuro (future more solid) program. Sometimes it is referred to as Coyote. This weapon was introduced in 2008.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Albania, Algeria, Egypt, Iraq, Italy, Kazakhstan, Mexico, Poland, Qatar, Romania, Turkmenistan, Ukraine.

Here is Variants information for the Beretta ARX-160 Italian 5.56 mm Assault Rifle:
The ARX160 A2 is The Beretta ARX160 A2, also referred to as the ARX160 SF (Special Forces) is similar to the ARX160 but features a shorter buttstock, an extended Picatinny rail on the bottom of its handguard and uses a 12-inch barrel..
The ARX160 A3 is The Beretta ARX160 A3 is an improved variant of the ARX160 which features some design changes. The changes includes a redesigned handguard, where its top row air vent slits have been replaced with a larger square cut outs, though the bottom row air vent still retains the thinner slits..
The ARX100 is The Beretta ARX100 is a semi-automatic only variant of the ARX160 intended for the civilian market..
The ARX160 .22 LR is The Beretta ARX160 .22 LR is a rimfire variant of the ARX100, chambered in .22 Long Rifle cartridge..
The ARX200 is In late 2015, Beretta introduced the ARX200 chambered in 7.62×51mm NATO cartridge..

Here is System information for the Beretta ARX-160 Italian 5.56 mm Assault Rifle:
The Alternate Designation is Beretta ARX-160.
The Type is Assault Rifle.
The Caliber is 5.56 mm.
The Manufacturer is Beretta.
The Action is Gas-operated rotating bolt.
The Rate of Fire is 700 rounds/min.
The Muzzle Velocity is 920 m/s.
The Effective Firing Range is 600 m.
The Feed System is 30-round detachable STANAG Magazine, 100-round detachable C-Mag drum magazine (5.56×45mm NATO).
The Sights is Back-up folding iron sights and integrated Picatinny rail for various optical sights.
