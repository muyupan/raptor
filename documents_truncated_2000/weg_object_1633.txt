This document describes the Yug-Class (Project 862 Class) Russian Hydrographic Survey Vessel.

Yug-Class (Project 862 Class) Russian Hydrographic Survey Vessel was placed in service between May of 1978 and Sept. 6, 1983. The class was built for "complex oceanographic research; exploration of the sea bed and sampling of soils; gravimetric studies; hydrographic and geophysical research, including the removal and planting of oceanographic buoys; a collection of navigational and hydrographic data; and inshore surveys by [use of the] embarked two Project 727 glass-reinforced plastic-hulled cutters."

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Russian Federation.
