This document describes the Negev NG-5 Israeli 5.56mm Light Machine Gun.

The Negev NG-5 Israeli 5.56mm Light Machine Gun is a light machine gun chambered in a 5.56×45mm NATO cartridge. It has a barrel length of 460 mm and two operation modes; semi-automatic for accurate and fast controlled fire, and fully automatic for maximum firepower.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Azerbaijan, Brazil, Cameroon, Colombia, Congo, Costa Rica, Cyprus, Equatorial Guinea, Estonia, Georgia, India, Israel, Ivory Coast, Kenya, Mexico, North Macedonia, Paraguay, Philippines, Senegal, Tanzania, Thailand, Ukraine, Viet Nam.

Here is System information for the Negev NG-5 Israeli 5.56mm Light Machine Gun:
The Alternate Designation is Negev NG-5.
The Type is Light Machine Gun.
The Caliber is 5.56 mm.
The Manufacturer is Israel Weapon Industries (IWI).
The Action is Gas operated, rotating bolt.
The Gas Regulator is The Negev's adjustable gas regulator has three settings:
setting "1" is used exclusively when feeding from a magazine (rate of fire in this mode is around 850–1,050 rounds per minute).
setting "2" is used in normal operating conditions when feeding from a belt (rate of fire in this mode is also around 850–1,050 rounds per minute).
setting "3" which is used under adverse operating conditions, such as in the presence of dust, dirt or heavy fouling (rate of fire in this mode is around 950–1,150 rounds per minute)..
The Rate of Fire is 850–1,050 rounds/min.
The Muzzle Velocity is 915 m/s.
The Maximum Firing Range is 1,200 m.
The Effective Firing Range is 300–1,000 m.
The Feed System is 150-, 200-round disintegrating M27 ammunition belt, 35-round box magazine or STANAG NATO magazines..
The Sights is Aperture with elevation drum, adjustable front post, folding tritium night sights, and a Picatinny rail for various optical sights.
