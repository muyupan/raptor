This document describes the Howa Type 89 Japanese 5.56mm Assault Rifle.

The Howa Type 89 Assault Rifle is a Japanese assault rifle used by the Japan Ground Self-Defense Forces (JGSDF), the Japan Coast Guard's Special Security Team (JCGSST), and the Special Assault Team (SAT). It has never been exported outside Japan due to strict anti-hardware export policies. It is known in the JGSDF service as "Buddy". The Type 89 was introduced to replace the Howa Type 64 rifle. The Type 89 is also able to fire the Type 06 rifle grenade. An under barrel grenade launcher cannot be fitted.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Japan.

Here is Variants information for the Howa Type 89 Japanese 5.56mm Assault Rifle:
The Type 89-F is The Type 89-F rifle is also known as the Type 89 Para, which has a folding stock compared to the fixed stock of the standard Type 89. The Type 89 Para is issued to airborne soldiers, tankers, recon soldiers, and armored infantry soldiers. The folding stock folds to the left side in order to not cover the safety selector on the right side of the weapon..
