This document describes the GC-45 Canadian 155mm Towed Gun-Howitzer.

The GC-45 (Gun, Canada, 45) is a 155 mm howitzer designed by Gerald Bull's Space Research Corporation (SRC) in the 1970s. Versions were produced by a number of companies during the 1980s, notably in Austria and South Africa.

The most publicized use of the design was in Iraq, where the GHN-45 variant used by some Iraqi artillery units had a longer range than any coalition cannon systems. This initially caused considerable worry on the part of the allied forces in the Persian Gulf War

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Austria, China, Iran (Islamic Republic of), Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Malaysia, Oman, Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, South Africa, Thailand, Turkey, United Arab Emirates.
