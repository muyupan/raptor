This document describes the KH-35 Russian Short-Range Anti-Ship Cruise Missile.

The Zvezda Kh-35 (Russian: Х-35 , AS-20 'Kayak') is a Soviet turbojet subsonic cruise anti-ship missile. The same missile can also be launched from helicopters, surface ships, and coastal defense batteries with the help of a rocket booster, in which case it is known as Uran ('Uranus', SS-N-25 'Switchblade', GRAU 3M24) or Bal ('Ball', SSC-6 'Sennight', GRAU 3K60). It is designed to attack vessels up to 5,000 tonnes.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Algeria, Azerbaijan, India, Iran (Islamic Republic of), Myanmar, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), Russian Federation, Ukraine, Venezuela (Bolivarian Republic of), Viet Nam.
