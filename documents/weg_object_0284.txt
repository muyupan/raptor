This document describes the Hwasong-6 (Scud-C) North Korean Short-Range Ballistic Missile.

The Hwasong-6 (Scud-C) North Korean Short-Range Ballistic Missile is the North Korean-produced variant of the Russian ‘Scud C’ and is a short-range ballistic missile. It has been in service with the DRPK rocket force since 1992.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Egypt, Iran (Islamic Republic of), Iraq, Libya, Myanmar, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), Syria, Viet Nam, Yemen.

Here is System information for the Hwasong-6 (Scud-C) North Korean Short-Range Ballistic Missile:
The Alternate Designation is Hwasong-6; NATO: Scud-C.
The Type is Short-Range Ballistic Missile.
The Manufacturer is 125 factory at Pyongyang, a research and development institute at Sanum-dong and the Musudan-ri Launch Facility..
The Launcher is Mobile truck transporter - erector.
The Crew is 5.
The Payload is Single warhead.
The Warhead is HE, chemical, biological, nuclear.
The Propulsion is Single-stage, liquid-fueled.
The Guidance System is Inertial system.
The Maximum Range is 500 km.
The CEP is INA.

Here is Dimensions information for the Hwasong-6 (Scud-C) North Korean Short-Range Ballistic Missile:
The Length is 10.94 m.
The Diameter is 0.88 m.
The Missile Weight is 6,095 kg.
The Warhead Weight is 700 – 770 kg.
