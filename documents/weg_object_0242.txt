This document describes the Indian Field Gun (IFG) Indian 105mm Towed Howitzer.

The Indian Field Gun Indian 105mm Towed Howitzer was designed and developed by the Armament Research and Development Establishment (ARDE) in 1972 to replace the 25-pounder guns used by the Indian Army.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: India, Myanmar.

Here is System information for the Indian Field Gun (IFG) Indian 105mm Towed Howitzer:
The Alternative Designation is Indian Field Gun; IFG.
The Type is Towed Howitzer.
The Caliber is 105 mm.
The Carriage is split trail.
The Crew is 8.
The Elevation is -5° to +73°.
The Traverse is 30° Left & Right.
The Muzzle Velocity is 475 m/s.
The Maximum Firing Range is 17,200 - 20,000 m.

Here is Dimensions information for the Indian Field Gun (IFG) Indian 105mm Towed Howitzer:

The Barrel Length is 2.31 m.
The Width is 2.21 m.
The Height is 1.73 m.
The Weight, Combat is 2,380 kg.
