This document describes the BOV M11 Serbian 4x4 Armored Reconnaissance Vehicle.

The BOV M11 is part of the BOV family of light armored vehicles. The M11 is a 4x4 all-wheel drive vehicle specialized in reconnaissance. It is an armored vehicle manufactured in SDPR "Complex Battle Systems" factory in Velika Plana, Serbia.

The BOV M11 has a primary role as a reconnaissance vehicle and command-reconnaissance vehicle. If used by artillery units it could be a remote observation post that observes the enemy and guides firing. It has a crew of 3, including a driver, commander, and gunner. Has to room for four more personnel that could include based on concrete mission scouts and the artillery command platoon’s CO. The vehicle has four-wheel drive and is powered by a diesel engine developing 190 hp. It has many special reconnaissance systems and artillery systems built in based on a concrete mission role. The driver has thermal camera sight at the front and a CCD TV camera at the rear. It is armed with a 12.7 mm remote-controlled weapon station for day and night combat and fire on the move and stands. It is possible to integrate another RCWS with a 7.62mm gun and for a more recce combat role RCWS with a 30 mm grenade launcher BGA-30 or M10 RCWS with a 20mm gun and 7.62mm coaxial gun.

Equipment for artillery reconnaissance vehicles includes 1. Artillery electronic direction finder (AEG) 2. Communications equipment 3. Artillery battery FCS computer

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Bangladesh, Kenya, Serbia.
