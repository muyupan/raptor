This document describes the 2S19M1 (Msta-SM1) Russian 152mm Self-Propelled Howitzer (SPH).

The  2S19M1 (Msta-SM1) Russian 152mm Self-Propelled Howitzer (SPH) Improved version with an automatic laying system and Glonass. Upgrade to the 2S19 Msta-s. The 2S19 "Msta-S" (Russian: Мста, after the Msta River) is a 152.4 mm self-propelled howitzer designed by Russia/Soviet Union, which entered service in 1989 as the successor to the 2S3 Akatsiya. The vehicle is based on the T-80 tank hull but is powered by the T-72's diesel engine.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Russian Federation.

Here is Variants information for the 2S19M1 (Msta-SM1) Russian 152mm Self-Propelled Howitzer (SPH):
The 152 mm howitzer 2A65 is A towed version of the same gun..
The 1K17 Szhatie - is A "laser tank" armed with a battery of lasers meant to disable optoelectronic systems; uses Msta-S chassis and turret..
The 2S33 "Msta-SM" is An Improved version. Prototype..
The 2S19M1 (2000) is An Improved version with automatic laying system and Glonass..
The 2S19M2 "Msta-SM2" (2013) is An Improved version currently in production equipped with a new automatic fire control system which increases the rate of fire. Digital electronic maps are now available which significantly speeds up the terrain orientation in difficult geographical conditions and allows performing faster and more efficiently firing missions..
The 2S19M1-155 (2006) is A 155mm export version of the 2S19M1, fitted with an L/52 gun with a range of 40+ km..
The 2S27 "Msta-K" is Wheeled variant (K = kolyosnij), based on a 8x8 truck chassis. There were several different prototypes, including one based on a KrAZ-ChR-3130 and two based on the Ural-5323 (with and without turret)..
The 2S30 "Iset" is Improved version, prototype only..
The 2S35 "Koalitsiya-SV" - is Project for a new artillery system for the Russian land forces (SV = sukhoputniye voyska). Early prototypes consisted of a 2S19 chassis with a modified turret, fitted with an over-and-under dual autoloaded 152mm howitzer. Development of this variant was abandoned in favor of an entirely new artillery system using the same designation..
