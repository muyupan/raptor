This document describes the UR-77 Meteorit Russian Mine Clearing Vehicle.

The UR-77 Meteorit (Russian: УР-77 Метеорит, lit. 'Meteorite') is a Soviet mine clearing vehicle, based on a variant of the 2S1 Gvozdika chassis. The vehicle is armed with a launcher and two mine-clearing line charges. When launched, a charge causes a shock wave that destroys or disables all the shells or mines along the area of the line charge. (with a width of 6 metres and length up to 90 metres).

The vehicle has also been used offensively, where its line charge has been used to destroy entire streets in urban combat.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Azerbaijan, CFE Treaty, Russian Federation, Syria, Ukraine.
