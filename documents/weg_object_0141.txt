This document describes the SNR-75 (Fan Song) Russian Fire Control and Tracking Radar.

The SNR-75 (Fan Song) Russian Fire Control and Tracking Radar are capable of tracking a single target at a time, and can guide up to three missiles at once to it.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Angola, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bulgaria, China, Cuba, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran (Islamic Republic of), Kyrgyzstan, Libya, Mongolia, Myanmar, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), Pakistan, Romania, Russian Federation, Sudan, Syria, Tajikistan, Viet Nam, Yemen, Zimbabwe.

Here is System information for the SNR-75 (Fan Song) Russian Fire Control and Tracking Radar:
The Alternative Designation is SNR-75; NATO: Fan Song.
The Type is Fire Control and Tracking Radar.
The Crew is INA.
The Frequency is E/F band, G band.
The Search Pulse Repetition Frequency (PRF) is 828-1,440 Hz (E/F Band).
The Tracking Pulse Repetition Frequency (PRF) is 1,656-2,880 Hz (G band).
The Beam Width is 10x2deg (E/F band);
7.5x1.5deg (G band).
The Pulse Width is 0.4-1.2 μs (G band).
The Maximum Range is 60-120 km (E/F band);
75-145 km (G band).
The Power is 600 kW (E/F band);
1.0 MW (G band).
The Note is The radars feature two orthogonal antennas, one for azimuth and one for elevation, which can operate in a track-while-scan mode. These antennas transmit 10 × 2 degree or 7.5 x 1.5 degree beams and perform a 'flapping' motion as they scan their sectors..
