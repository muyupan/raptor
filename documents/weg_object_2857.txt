This document describes the BRDM 2U Russian 4x4 Command and Control Vehicle.

The BRDM-2U command vehicle has no turret but carries a generator and extra radios. The increased number of antennas marks this version as a command and control vehicle. It is found in battalion and regimental headquarters of maneuver units and also is used extensively by artillery units.
The BRDM-2U (BRDM-2UM) carries a 1 kW generator and extra radios (two R-123 and an R-107, or according to other sources one R-105M, one R-108M and one R-130M). On either side of the upper hull there is a big antenna base.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Afghanistan, Algeria, Angola, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Cambodia, Cameroon, Cuba, Czech Republic, Djibouti, Egypt, Eritrea, Estonia, Georgia, Germany, Guinea, Hungary, India, Indonesia, Iraq, Israel, Ivory Coast, Kyrgyzstan, Laos (People's Democratic Republic), Latvia, Libya, Lithuania, Macedonia, Madagascar, Maldives, Mali, Mongolia, Myanmar, Namibia, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), Pakistan, Palestine, Peru, Poland, Romania, Russian Federation, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, Tajikistan, Tanzania, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, United States of America, Viet Nam, Zambia, Zimbabwe.
