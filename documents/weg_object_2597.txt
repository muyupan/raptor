This document describes the BTR-40 Russian 4x4 Armored Personnel Carrier (APC).

The BTR-40 is a Soviet non-amphibious, wheeled armored personnel carrier and reconnaissance vehicle. It is often referred to as the Sorokovka in Soviet service. It is also the first mass-produced Soviet APC. It was eventually replaced in the APC role by the BTR-152 and in the scout car role by the BRDM-1.

The BTR-40's design was based on the GAZ-63 four wheel drive truck which went into production in 1946. The design featured a self-bearing body which was a new feature in Soviet vehicles. The hull has two side doors for the commander and driver and a back door. The vehicle can transport up to eight fully equipped soldiers or 1 tonne of cargo.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Burundi, Cuba, Guinea-Bissau, Indonesia, Israel, Laos (People's Democratic Republic), Mali, North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), Rwanda, Tanzania, Viet Nam, Yemen.
