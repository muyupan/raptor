This document describes the SandCat Gen 2 Israeli 4x4 Light Armored Vehicle.

SandCat, Plasan’s family of 4X4 armored vehicles, offers the flexibility and robustness needed for every defense and security mission. While providing high-end protection, SandCat maintains its maneuverability and agility, even when equipped with surveillance and communications systems. The SandCat family has been designed to support a wide range of challenges and missions including: law enforcement, Special Forces, homeland security, border patrol and armed conflicts. SandCats are also used to transport troops, as a command and control centers, and to transport VIPs though conflict zones. To date, there are over 15 variants of SandCats, tailored to meet specific field conditions.

This equipment has proliferated into the following countries: Azerbaijan, Bulgaria, Canada, CFE Treaty, Colombia, Israel, Kazakhstan, Mexico, Nigeria, Poland, Romania, South Korea (Republic of Korea), Sweden, Ukraine, United States of America.

Here is Variants information for the SandCat Gen 2 Israeli 4x4 Light Armored Vehicle:
The SandCat Gen 1 is The SandCat Gen 1 is the original variant of the SandCat family. Very few of the vehicles were produced and the main users are the Swedish police and some elements of Israel’s special forces and border forces..
The SandCat Gen 2 is The SandCat Gen 2 series was really the first production standard SandCat. It is similar to the Gen 1 and features moderate changes to the automotive components and external appearance..
The SandCat Gen 3 is The SandCat Gen 3 followed an upgrade to the commercial chassis and was originally aimed at police force, it improves on the payload of the Gen 2 platforms and mobility and the chassis can be used to accommodate alternative weapon systems..
The SandCat, Tank Destroyer is The SandCat Gen 3 can be configured – in its flatbed configuration – to perform as a tank destroyer. For this the rear of the vehicle is modified to accommodate a guided missile launcher and storage for additional missiles..
The SandCat, Indirect fire support is The SandCat Gen 3 can also be configured – in its flatbed configuration – as an indirect fire support vehicle. For this role it is fitted with a mortar that is mounted on the flatbed at the rear of the hull. The mortar fires over the rear of the vehicle and the cab is retained from the Gen 3 design..
The SandCat Granite is The IDF’s SandCat Granite is a Gen2 SandCat fitted out with an ELTA-supplied intelligence gathering sensor suite..
